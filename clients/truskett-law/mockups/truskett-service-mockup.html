What Personal Injury Law Covers

Personal injury law gives people a legal path to seek compensation when someone else’s fault causes harm. That fault can belong to a driver, a property owner, a nursing home, a manufacturer, a surgeon, or another party whose carelessness caused the injury.

How the Process Works

Personal injury cases follow a general path. Knowing the steps helps reduce uncertainty.

1. Initial case review
You describe what happened. The attorney reviews the facts and explains whether a viable claim exists. There is no cost for this conversation.

2. Investigation
The firm gathers evidence. This can include accident reports, medical records, photos, witness statements, and expert input depending on the type of case.

3. Demand and negotiation
Once your medical treatment has stabilized, the firm sends a demand to the at-fault party’s insurer. Most cases resolve through negotiation at this stage.

4. Litigation if needed
If the insurance company refuses a fair offer, the firm is prepared to file suit and take the case to trial. Having a trial lawyer involved from the start affects how insurers evaluate your claim.

5. Resolution
When a settlement is reached or a verdict is returned, funds are distributed after attorney fees and case costs are deducted. You will know exactly what to expect before you sign anything.

No Fee Unless You Recover

Truskett Law handles personal injury cases on a contingency fee arrangement. You do not pay attorney’s fees out of pocket. If no money is recovered, you owe no fee. This structure means the firm’s interests are aligned with yours from day one.

Why the Insurance Company Is Not on Your Side

Insurance adjusters are trained to settle claims for as little as possible. They may contact you quickly after an accident, ask for a recorded statement, or offer a fast settlement before you know the full extent of your injuries.

Accepting an early offer or giving a recorded statement without legal guidance can reduce or eliminate what you are able to recover later. Speaking with an attorney before you respond to an insurer costs you nothing and can protect your options.

What to Do After an Injury

The steps you take in the days after an accident affect your case. Follow these if you can.

  • Get medical attention right away, even if you feel okay initially. Some injuries appear days later.
  • Report the incident. File a police report for vehicle crashes or an incident report for a fall at a business.
  • Document everything. Photos of the scene, your injuries, and any property damage help establish what happened.
  • Keep records. Save all medical bills, prescriptions, and any communication from insurers.
  • Avoid giving recorded statements to the other party’s insurance company without speaking to an attorney first.
